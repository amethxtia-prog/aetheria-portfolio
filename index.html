Case Study 01

Mycelium Mind

Healing Garden System

Overview: Mycelium Mind is a biomimetic healing garden concept inspired by the intelligence of mycelial networks. The project translates interconnected natural systems into a spatial experience centered on pause, emotional regulation, and gentle movement.

Timeline
2026
Role
Research, concept development, spatial design
Focus
Wellbeing, community, biomimicry
Tools
Rhino, rendering, visual storytelling

Problem: Urban environments often prioritize efficiency over emotional wellbeing, leaving few public spaces that actively invite reflection, calm, and restorative interaction.

Concept: I looked to mycelium as a model for connection, resilience, and quiet support. Rather than designing a linear park experience, I created a system of circular and branching pathways that encourage non-linear movement, discovery, and moments of pause.

Process: The project developed through site planning, biomimetic form exploration, and pavilion studies. Petal-like structures became the primary spatial language, offering zones for meditation, reflection, swings, resting, and social gathering. The goal was to create a landscape that feels emotionally protective while still remaining open and communal.

Outcome: The final proposal frames the garden as a living system of care — one that supports emotional resilience through natural metaphors, soft form language, and experiential storytelling.

Why these functions and forms

The program was organized around emotional states rather than only physical activities. Each zone was designed to support a different kind of pause: meditation for quiet reflection, resting for stillness, swings for gentle movement, and the social area for soft interaction.

The petal-like canopies were chosen to feel protective without being closed. Their curved form creates shelter while maintaining openness, light, and connection to nature.

The non-linear path system reflects how healing is not linear. Instead of forcing a route, the space allows wandering, choice, and emotional pacing.

Case Study 02

Land Meets the Sun

Contemporary Filipiniana Gown

Overview: Land Meets the Sun is a contemporary Filipiniana concept that reinterprets heritage through material storytelling, woven structure, and symbolic color. The project explores how tradition can evolve without losing its cultural grounding.

Timeline
2026
Role
Concept, illustration, material direction
Focus
Culture, craft, storytelling
Themes
Identity, sunlight, land, heritage

Problem: Traditional garments are often preserved visually, but are not always reimagined in ways that reflect contemporary identity, emotional symbolism, or evolving material language.

Concept: I translated the idea of land meeting the sun into silhouette, embroidery, and woven sleeve structure. The gown combines softness and ceremony: floral detailing reflects life and growth, while the green woven sleeves and central panel symbolize land, continuity, and rootedness.

Process: The design was developed through sketching, symbolic mapping, and refinement of key visual elements. I focused on how the Filipiniana silhouette could remain recognizable while introducing a more narrative-driven, poetic direction through color, texture, and proportion.

Outcome: The final concept creates a bridge between cultural preservation and modern expression. It presents the Filipiniana as both a garment and a storytelling object — honoring heritage while inviting reinterpretation.

Case Study 03

Unextinguished

Light Exhibit for Persian Women

Overview: Unextinguished is an immersive light exhibit concept honoring the resilience, visibility, and creative voices of Persian women and artists. The project uses atmosphere, draped material, and spatial composition to create a reflective emotional environment.

Timeline
2026
Role
Exhibit concept, render direction, narrative framing
Focus
Advocacy, atmosphere, storytelling
Themes
Voice, remembrance, presence, resilience

Problem: Stories of resilience and resistance often lack physical spaces where they can be encountered collectively, emotionally, and with care.

Concept: I approached the exhibit as an embodied spatial narrative. Rather than relying on direct explanation, the installation uses light, translucency, and softness to evoke absence, presence, and quiet strength. Draped materials create a layered threshold experience, while the central structure acts as a symbolic focal point.

Process: The project evolved through exhibition references, mood-building, and atmospheric rendering. I explored how softness and light could hold political and emotional weight without becoming visually aggressive. The composition was shaped to feel intimate, reflective, and sacred.

Outcome: The final proposal transforms advocacy into an immersive encounter. It creates space not only for viewing, but for feeling — allowing the audience to move through memory, attention, and collective reflection.
